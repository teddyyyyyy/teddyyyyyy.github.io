## About Me

Hi, I’m **Teddy Chen**, a Medical AI Researcher based in Taiwan, with a Master’s degree in Artificial Intelligence from **Royal Holloway, University of London**.

I specialize in building reliable machine learning systems, with a focus on **medical imaging and AI system deployment**. My recent work investigates hidden data leakage in clinical AI, showing how improper validation can significantly overestimate model performance, and proposing more realistic evaluation frameworks.

I have hands-on experience across the full ML lifecycle — from large-scale data curation and preprocessing to model training, evaluation, and deployment using FastAPI and Docker. My work focuses on **computer vision and machine learning**, where I build models for real-world applications and ensure their reliability through careful validation and analysis.

I place strong emphasis on **robust evaluation**, applying techniques such as cross-validation and statistical testing to ensure models generalize beyond controlled settings—especially in medical imaging tasks.

In addition, I work with large language models (e.g., LLaMA, Qwen), integrating them into production systems for tasks such as medical report generation. I design hybrid pipelines and safety mechanisms to improve reliability and reduce hallucination in practical deployments.

My interests lie in **AI reliability, generalization, and production-ready ML systems**.
